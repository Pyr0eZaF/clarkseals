Valve Seals

A valve seal is a critical sealing component used to prevent the leakage of fluids or gases around valve stems and internal valve components. Commonly found in engines and industrial systems, valve seals ensure efficient operation by maintaining proper pressure and preventing oil or fluid from entering combustion chambers or other sealed areas. Made from durable materials like rubber, PTFE, or metal composites, valve seals are designed to withstand high temperatures, pressures, and chemical exposure for long-term reliability and performance.
