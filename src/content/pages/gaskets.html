Gaskets

A gasket is a mechanical sealing component used to fill the space between two or more mating surfaces, preventing leakage of fluids or gases under compression. Typically made from materials such as rubber, silicone, metal, or composite materials, gaskets are essential for maintaining tight, reliable seals in engines, pumps, pipes, and industrial equipment. They are designed to withstand a range of pressures, temperatures, and chemical exposures, ensuring long-term performance and system integrity.
